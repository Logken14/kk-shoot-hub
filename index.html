Who is MacMacとは

The first push for an international audience 国際市場への最初の挑戦

I joined Kawanishi Kaban because I genuinely believe in what they're building — bags made to last, designed with purpose, in one of Japan's most overlooked prefectures.川西鞄に加わったのは、彼らが作るものに本当に信念を感じたからです。長く使えるバッグ、目的を持ったデザイン、日本でも見過ごされがちな県から生まれるプロダクト。

My role is to use my passion for videography to bring Kawanishi Kaban to the English-speaking world — Canada, America, Europe — audiences who haven't found this brand yet but absolutely should.私の役割は映像への情熱を活かして、川西鞄を英語圏の世界へ届けること。カナダ、アメリカ、ヨーロッパ — まだこのブランドを知らない、でも絶対に知るべき人たちへ。

Every video is a translation — of craft into culture, of Japanese attention to detail into something the world can feel.すべての動画は翻訳です。職人技を文化へ、日本のこだわりを世界が感じられるものへ。

Mission & Contentミッション＆コンテンツ

Making craftsmanship visible to the world 職人技を世界に見える化する

"In one of the smallest, most overlooked places in Japan — a team quietly making some of the most thoughtful bags in the world." 「日本で最も小さく、見過ごされた場所で — チームが静かに世界で最も思慮深いバッグを作っている。」

The details nobody sees — bonded seams, Tuscan leather handles, interior finishing — made visible through close-up cinematic shots.誰も見ないディテール — 接着縫製、トスカーナレザーのハンドル、内装の仕上げ — シネマティックなクローズアップで可視化する。

The Mistake Philosophy
Tue Jun 3 · 8PM JST~40 secBrand Philosophy
▾
Hook — First 3 Seconds
"There's a bag that was sitting in a warehouse not selling. One change fixed everything."
■ Show the Minimon in frame 1 — closed, clean shot
Body

This is the Minimon. They had inventory piling up. Nobody was buying it.

Instead of discounting it or scrapping it — they just asked one question. What's actually wrong with it?

Turned out people wanted a zipper instead of a magnetic snap. They added the zipper. Now it sells.

I've been trying to make content for nine months and struggling with the same thing. Different angles, different formats, different ideas. And watching this company just — change one thing and move forward — that actually helped me. Change the angle. Not the goal.

Visual Notes
  1. Frame 1: Minimon on clean surface, zipper in focus
  2. Cut to: you holding it, demonstrating zipper vs magnetic
  3. Cut to: quick montage of you filming different angles
  4. End: you on camera, genuine

Body

When this company first started they were only making bags for other brands. Their name wasn't on anything. Just producing, shipping, moving on.

At some point the founder made a decision — I want to be responsible for what I make. I want my name on it. Not for ego. Because if your name is on something, you actually care about whether it lasts.

The Anti-Gravity Backpack
Sun Jun 15 · 8PM JST~50 secProduct Showcase
▾
Hook — First 3 Seconds
"This bag changed how I think about carrying things."
■ VISUAL HOOK: Backpack on table, slow zoom out
Body

Every bag I've used my whole life puts the heaviest items at the bottom. This one does the opposite on purpose.

When weight sits low it pulls your body away from center. You end up compensating without even realizing it.

This backpack puts the heavy stuff up top — closer to your center of gravity. The bag sits with you instead of against you.

I didn't fully believe it until I took it on a four hour hike up a mountain. It's not a hiking bag. But me and everyone else on that trail all said the same thing — this felt way more manageable than it had any right to. The anti-gravity thing actually works.

Visual Notes
  1. Open: backpack on table, zoom out slowly
  2. Cut to: demonstration — heavy item going in at the top
  3. Cut to: you wearing it, side profile showing how it sits
  4. Cut to: trail or outdoor path — natural carry
  5. End: you on camera, direct

Caption Formulaキャプション構成
1
Hookフック
Visual hook or paradox — 12 words max. Specific number + unexpected verb wins. e.g. "12 pockets. Zero excuses."視覚的フックまたは逆説 — 最大12語。具体的な数字＋意外な動詞が勝つ。例：「12個のポケット。言い訳なし。」
2–3
Product + Place製品＋場所
Product name + 1 spec + place (always include Kagawa or Takamatsu). e.g. "The Grabe. 13 pockets. Takamatsu, Japan."製品名＋仕様1つ＋場所（香川または高松を必ず含める）。例：「グラベ。13個のポケット。香川県高松市。」
4
Emotion感情
Emotional reason to own it — embed searchable keywords here naturally. e.g. "Built for the person who buys once and keeps it forever."所有する感情的な理由 — ここに検索キーワードを自然に埋め込む。例：「一度買ってずっと使い続ける人のために作られた。」
5
CTACTA
Rotate: Shop / Save / Engage. e.g. "Save this for your next bag search."ローテーション：購入/保存/エンゲージ。例：「次のバッグ探しのために保存を。」
6
Hashtagsハッシュタグ
3–5 max. Always-on 4 + 1 rotating. In caption not comments.最大3〜5個。常時4個＋ローテーション1個。コメントではなくキャプション内に。
